Hindsight bias	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">People’s tendency after learning about a given outcome to be overconfident about whether they could have predicted that outcome.</span></div></b>"
Hypothesis	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A prediction about what will happen under particular circumstances.</span></div></b>"
Theory	A set of related propositions intended to describe some phenomenon or aspect of the world
Correlational research	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Research that involves measuring two or more variables and assessing whether there is a relationship between them.</span></div></b>"
Experimental research	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">In social psychology, research that randomly assigns people to different conditions, or situations, enabling researchers to make strong inferences about why a relationship exists or how different situations affect behavior.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Third variable	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A variable, often unmeasured in correlational research that can be the true explanation for the relationship between two other variables.</span></div></b>"
Self-selection	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">In correlational research, the situation in which the participant, rather than the researcher, determines the participant’s level of each variable (for example, how many hours per day they spend playing video games or whether or not they are married), thereby creating the problem that unknown other properties might be responsible for the observed relationship.</span></div></b>"
Longitudinal study	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A study conducted at different points in time with the same participants.</span></div></b>"
Independent variable	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">In correlational research, this variable is measured. In experimental research, this variable is manipulated; it is hypothesized to be the cause of a particular outcome.</span></div></b>"
Dependent variable	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">In experimental research, the variable that is measured (as opposed to manipulated); it is hypothesized to be affected by manipulation of the independent variable.</span></div></b>"
Control condition	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A condition comparable to the experimental condition in every way except that it lacks the one ingredient hypothesized to produce the expected effect on the dependent variable.</span></div></b>"
Random assignment	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Assigning participants in experimental research to different conditions randomly, so they are as likely to be assigned to one condition as to another, with the effect of making the types of people in the different conditions roughly equal.</span></div></b>"
Natural experiment	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A naturally occurring event or phenomenon with somewhat different conditions (e.g., before versus after) that can be compared with almost as much rigor as conditions manipulated by the investigator in an experiment.</span></div></b>"
External validity	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">How well the results of a study generalize to contexts outside the conditions of the laboratory.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Field experiment	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">An experiment conducted in the real world (not a lab), usually with participants who are not aware that they are in a study of any kind.</span></div></b>"
Internal validity	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">In experimental research, confidence that only the manipulated variable could have produced the results.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Reliability	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The degree to which the particular way researchers measure a given variable is likely to yield consistent results.&nbsp;</span></b>"
Measurement validity	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The correlation between a measure and some outcome the measure is supposed to predict.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Statistical significance	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A measure of the probability that a given result could have occurred by chance.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Replication	Reproduction of research results by the original investigator or by someone else
Open science	Practices such as sharing data and research material with anyone in the broader scientific community in an effort to increase the integrity and replicability of scientific research.
Institutional review board (IRB)	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A committee that examines research proposals and makes judgements about the ethical appropriateness of the research.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Informed consent	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A person’s signed agreement to participate in a procedure or research sturdy after learning all of its relevant aspects.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Deception research	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Research in which the participants are misled about the purpose of the research or the meaning of something that is done to them.</span></div></b>"
Debriefing	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">In preliminary versions of an experiment, asking participants directly if they understood the instructions, found the setup to be reasonable, and so on. After an experiment, debriefing is used to educate participants about the questions being studied.</span></div></b>"
Basic science	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Science or research concerned with trying to understand some phenomenon in its own right, with a view toward using that understanding to build valid theories about the nature of some aspect of the world.</span></div></b>"
Applied science	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Science or research concerned with solving important real-world problems.</span></div></b>"
Intervention	"An effort to change a person's behavior"
Elaboration likelihood model (ELM)	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A model of persuasion that maintains there are two routes to persuasion: the central route and the peripheral route.</span></b>"
Central route	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A route to persuasion wherein people think carefully and deliberately about the content of a persuasive message, attending to its logic and the strength of its arguments as well as to related evidence and principles.</span></b>"
Peripheral route	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A route to persuasion wherein people attend to relatively easy-to-process, superficial cues related to a persuasive message, such as its length or the expertise or attractiveness of the source of the message.</span></b>"
Source characteristics	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Characteristics of the person who delivers a persuasive message, such as attractiveness, credibility, and certainty.</span></b>"
Sleeper effect	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">An effect that occurs when a persuasive message from an unreliable source initially exerts little influence but later causes attitudes to shift.</span></b>"
Message characteristics	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Aspects or content of a persuasive message, including the quality of evidence and the explicitness of its conclusions.</span></div></b>"
Identifiable victim effect	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The tendency to be more moved by the vivid plight of a single individual than by the struggles of a more abstract number of people.</span></b>"
Audience characteristics	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Characteristics of those who receive a persuasive message including need for cognition, mood, and age.</span></b>"
Agenda control	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Efforts by the media to emphasize certain events and topics, thereby shaping which issues and events people think are important.</span></b>"
Hostile media phenomenon	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The tendency for people to see media coverage as biased against their own side and in favor of their opponents’ side.</span></b>"
Thought polarization hypothesis	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The hypothesis that more extended thought about a particular issue tends to produce a more extreme, entrenched attitude</span></b>"
Attitude inoculation	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Small attacks on people’s beliefs that engage their preexisting attitudes, prior commitments, and background knowledge, enabling them to counteract a subsequent larger attack and thus resist persuasion.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
General aggression model	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A broad approach to understanding the causes of aggression through a focus on situational factors, construal factors, and biological and cultural contributions.</span></div></b>"
Hostile aggression	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Behavior intended to harm another person, either physically or psychologically, and motivated by feelings of anger and hostility.</span></b>"
Instrumental aggression	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">Behavior intended to harm another person in the service of motives other than pure hostility (such as attracting attention, acquiring wealth, or advancing political or ideological causes).</span></div></b>"
Dehumanization	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The attribution of nonhuman characteristics and denial of human qualities to groups other than one’s own.</span></div></b>"
Culture of honor	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A culture defined by its members’ strong concerns about their own and others’ reputations, leading to sensitivity to insults and a willingness to use violence to avenge any perceived slight.</span></b>"
Rape-prone culture	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">A culture in which rape tends to be used as an act of war against enemy women, as a ritual act, or as a threat against women to keep them subservient to men.</span></b>"
Inclusive fitness	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">According to evolutionary theory, an individual’s reproductive success ensures the transmission of an individual’s genes to future generations.</span></b>"
Precarious manhood hypothesis	"<b><div><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The idea that a man’s gender identity, which significantly involves strength and toughness, may be lost&nbsp; under various conditions and that such a loss can trigger aggressive behavior.&nbsp;</span></div></b>"
Reactive devaluation	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The tendency to attach less value to an offer in a negotiation once the opposing group makes it.</span></b>"
Restorative justice	"<b><span style=""color: rgb(32, 33, 36); background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); font-weight: 400;"">The process of having perpetrators of harm take responsibility and apologize to those they’ve harmed and for those individuals to express their grievances.&nbsp;</span></b>"
